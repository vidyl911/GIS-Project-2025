Air Quality Mapping- Slovenia

This project investigates air pollution patterns across Slovenia by visualizing PM2.5 and PM10 concentration levels. Utilizing satellite and geospatial data, the study aims to identify spatial trends and support environmental decision-making.

Understanding Air Pollution

Air pollution is a critical environmental challenge, particularly in urban and industrial regions. This project focuses on mapping and analyzing key particulate matter indicators, PM2.5 and PM10, across the region using geospatial data. Through spatial visualization and data-driven insights, we aim to support informed policy-making and environmental health monitoring.

Study Area: Slovenia

Slovenia, a Central European country with diverse topography and climatic zones, provides an ideal case for studying air pollution patterns. Urban centers, industrial zones, and valleys surrounded by the Alps create micro-environments with varying pollution levels. This project captures these spatial variations and their implications on public health.

Overview of Particulate Matter Concentrations in Slovenia

PM2.5 Concentration

PM2.5 refers to fine inhalable particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers. These particles pose serious health risks as they penetrate deep into lungs and bloodstream.

PM10 Concentration

PM10 includes particles smaller than 10 micrometers. Though less fine than PM2.5, they still impact respiratory health and environmental quality.

Analysis Overview

10-Year Analysis

Multi-Pollutant Study

Focused analysis on PM2.5 and PM10 concentrations across Slovenian landscapes.

Population Impact

Assessment of exposure risks for densely populated Slovenian regions using integrated population data.

Data Sources & Specifications

  • Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service (CAMS): Provides gridded atmospheric pollutant concentrations (PM2.5, PM10) from advanced satellite models, crucial for regional air quality assessments.
  • Sentinel-5P (TROPOMI): Offers high-resolution tropospheric trace gas measurements, enabling detailed mapping of pollutant distributions over Slovenia.
  • WorldPop: Supplies gridded population data to assess exposure, allowing for precise calculation of health impacts in densely populated areas.
  • European Environment Agency (EEA): Delivers validated ground-based observations from monitoring stations to support calibration and quality control of remote sensing data.

Background and Significance

Slovenia's air quality varies drastically due to its complex terrain and urban development. Understanding these spatial disparities helps support public health policies and guide future environmental actions. This study contributes a detailed geospatial dataset for long-term air pollution monitoring.
